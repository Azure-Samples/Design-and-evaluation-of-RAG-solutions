QuickConnect Customer Service Guide: Handling Inquiries about Fixed Wireless Internet

Introduction

Fixed Wireless Internet is a type of internet service that delivers data wirelessly from the provider to the end user, bypassing the need for traditional wired connections like DSL or fiber optics. This guide aims to equip you, as a QuickConnect customer service agent, with the necessary information to handle inquiries related to fixed wireless internet effectively.

Key Features of Fixed Wireless Internet

  • High Speed: Capable of providing high-speed internet access, suitable for everyday activities such as browsing, streaming, and online gaming.
  • No Wired Infrastructure: Eliminates the need for extensive cabling and infrastructure, making it a viable option in areas where wired internet is impractical.
  • Ease of Installation: Generally quick and easy to install, often without the need for a technician visit.
  • Portability: Can be more portable compared to traditional wired solutions, allowing it to be relocated more easily.

Common Questions and Answers

1. What is Fixed Wireless Internet and how does it work?

Fixed Wireless Internet uses radio signals between two fixed points to deliver internet service. A transmitter is installed at a nearby location, which sends and receives data to and from a small antenna installed at the customer's premises.

2. Is Fixed Wireless Internet available in my area?

Availability of fixed wireless internet can vary widely by location. To check availability, follow these steps:

  1. Ask for the customer's address and postal code.
  2. Access our coverage map tool or internal database.
  3. Input the customer's details to see if fixed wireless internet is offered at their location.
  4. Inform the customer of the result and, if available, discuss plan options.

Installation Process

The installation process for fixed wireless internet is streamlined to ensure minimal disruption. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Site Survey: A preliminary site survey is conducted to determine the best placement for the antenna and ensure an unobstructed line of sight to the transmitter.
  2. Scheduling Installation: Once the site survey is completed, an installation appointment is scheduled at the customer’s convenience.
  3. Installing Equipment:
    • The technician installs the antenna on a fixed structure such as a rooftop or exterior wall.
    • A modem is connected to the antenna via a coaxial or Ethernet cable.
    • The modem is then connected to the customer’s devices, either directly or via a wireless router.
  4. Testing and Activation: The technician performs tests to ensure optimal signal strength and activates the service. The customer is given a demonstration of the system’s functionalities.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Here are some common issues customers might experience with fixed wireless internet and steps to troubleshoot:

1. Signal Interference

Interference can be caused by physical obstacles like buildings or trees, or other wireless devices. To troubleshoot:

  • Advise the customer to check if there are any new obstacles around the antenna.
  • Ensure that the antenna and transmitter have a clear line of sight.
  • Reposition the modem or router to avoid interference from other electronic devices.

2. Slow Internet Speeds

Several factors can contribute to slow internet speeds:

  • Check if the customer is exceeding their data cap, which could result in throttled speeds.
  • Advise the customer to restart their modem and router.
  • Ask the customer to disconnect some devices to ensure that a single device can test the speed.
  • Use speed test tools to measure current speeds and compare them to the customer's plan.

3. Intermittent Connectivity

For issues with intermittent connectivity, suggest the following steps:

  • Verify that the antenna is securely mounted and hasn’t been moved or damaged.
  • Check for firmware updates on the modem or router.
  • Ensure that the customer’s devices are within the range of their wireless network.
  • Run diagnostics to check for service interruptions or maintenance work in the area.

Escalation Procedures

If the customer’s issue cannot be resolved through standard troubleshooting, follow these escalation steps:

  1. Gather Detailed Information: Collect all relevant details about the issue, including any error messages, symptoms, and steps already taken to resolve it.
  2. Contact Technical Support: Reach out to our technical support team with the gathered information. Ensure you provide a comprehensive description to facilitate quicker resolution.
  3. Provide a Case Number: Give the customer a case number and inform them that their issue has been escalated to ensure follow-up and tracking.
  4. Follow Up: Follow up with both the technical support team and the customer to inform them of the status and any additional steps that may be required.

Billing and Plan Changes

Handling inquiries about billing or changes to plans related to fixed wireless internet involves specific steps:

Billing Inquiries

For billing-related questions:

  • Access the customer’s billing history and current plan details in our internal system.
  • Explain charges, due dates, and any applicable discounts or promotions.
  • Assist the customer with making payments, setting up auto-pay, or adjusting payment methods.
  • For disputes or unusual charges, escalate the issue to the billing department for further investigation.

Plan Changes

If the customer wishes to upgrade, downgrade, or cancel their plan:

  • Review the customer’s current plan and usage patterns.
  • Present the available options, detailing the benefits, costs, and any promotional offers applicable.
  • Confirm the customer’s choice and process the plan change in the system.
  • Inform the customer about any changes to their billing cycle or prorated charges for mid-cycle changes.
