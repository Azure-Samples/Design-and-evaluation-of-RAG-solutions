How to Reset a Customer's Modem

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for QuickConnect customer service agents to assist customers in resetting their modems. Resetting a modem can help resolve various technical issues, including slow internet speeds, connectivity problems, and firmware updates.

Pre-Check Before Resetting the Modem

Before resetting the customer's modem, follow these steps to ensure that a reset is necessary:

  1. Confirm Connectivity Issues:
    • Ask the customer about the specific issues they are experiencing (e.g., slow speeds, no connection, frequent disconnections).
    • Verify if other devices are experiencing the same issue.
  2. Check for Service Outages:
    • Access the QuickConnect service outage dashboard.
    • Ask the customer for their location details and check if there are any reported outages in their area.
  3. Inspect Modem and Connections:
    • Ask the customer to inspect the modem for any physical damage or loose connections.
    • Instruct the customer to ensure that all cables are securely connected.

Note: If the issue persists after completing the above steps, proceed with the reset process.

Performing a Soft Reset

A soft reset is a simple reboot of the modem and can often resolve minor connectivity issues without erasing any settings or configurations.

  1. Instruct the customer to locate the power cable of the modem.
  2. Ask the customer to unplug the power cable from the modem.
  3. Advise the customer to wait for approximately 30 seconds.
  4. Instruct the customer to plug the power cable back into the modem.
  5. Ask the customer to wait for the modem lights to stabilize, which may take 2-5 minutes.
Status Light Normal Indication
Power Light Solid Green
Internet Light Solid or Blinking Green
Wi-Fi Light Blinking Green

Note: If the modem lights do not stabilize or the issue persists, proceed with a hard reset.

Performing a Hard Reset

A hard reset will restore the modem to its factory default settings. This process will erase any customized settings, including Wi-Fi network names and passwords.

  1. Ask the customer to locate the reset button on the modem. It is usually a small, recessed button labeled 'Reset.' A pen or paperclip may be needed to press it.
  2. Instruct the customer to press and hold the reset button for at least 10-15 seconds using a pen or paperclip.
  3. Advise the customer to release the button and wait for the modem to reboot. This process may take several minutes.
  4. Once the modem has rebooted, ask the customer to check the status lights as outlined in the table above.
  5. Guide the customer to reconnect their devices to the modem’s Wi-Fi network. Inform them that they may need to use the default Wi-Fi name (SSID) and password provided on the modem’s label.

Post-Reset Procedures

After resetting the modem, assist the customer with the following steps to ensure their network is up and running:

  1. Reconnect Devices:
    • Walk the customer through reconnecting their devices to the Wi-Fi network using the default SSID and password.
    • Assist the customer in configuring any personalized settings (e.g., custom SSID and password).
  2. Firmware Updates:
    • Check if the modem requires any firmware updates.
    • Guide the customer through the firmware update process if necessary. Refer to the QuickConnect modem firmware update guide for detailed instructions.
  3. Verify Internet Connectivity:
    • Ask the customer to test their internet connection by browsing a few websites or running a speed test.
    • Confirm that all issues have been resolved and the connection is stable.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

If the customer continues to experience issues after resetting the modem, use the following troubleshooting steps:

  1. Check Connection Status:
    • Verify that all cables are securely connected and in good condition.
    • Ensure that the modem is properly connected to the service line.
  2. Inspect Modem Placement:
    • Instruct the customer to place the modem in a central location, away from obstructions and electronic devices that may cause interference.
  3. Change Wi-Fi Channel:
    • Guide the customer to access the modem settings via the web interface (usually by typing the modem’s IP address into a web browser).
    • Assist them in changing the Wi-Fi channel to reduce interference from nearby networks.
  4. Review Modem Logs:
    • If possible, review the modem logs for any error messages or unusual activity that may indicate the issue.
    • Refer to the QuickConnect modem log guide for detailed instructions on interpreting log entries.
